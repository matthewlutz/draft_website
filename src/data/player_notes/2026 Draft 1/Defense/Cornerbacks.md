## Mansoor Delane
LSU, 6'0 190

### Pros
- 22 Y/O - 4.44 40  
- Great football IQ 
- 26.2 passer rating allowed in 2025 (PFF)  
- 37.1% completion percentage allowed (PFF) 
- 11 forced incompletions in 2025 (PFF)  
- Prototype height and length  
- Elite fluidity 
- High level run defender
- Displays the ability to anticipate routes before they happen 
- Couples his prototypical length with elite technique to be truly sticky in man 
- Excelled at VT in a zone scheme, as well as LSU in a man scheme

### Cons 
- 5 missed tackles in 2025 
- Shows some over aggressiveness jumping routes 
- A bit on the slender side 
- Eyes sometime wander in zone coverage

## Colton Hood
Tennessee, 6'0 195, 20 Y/O

### Pros
- Good length
- Plays with fluid hips
- Good lateral mover
- Can play both man and zone at a high level
- Good short and long speed
- Plays with patience at the LOS 
- Press jam at the LOS regularly disrupts receivers timing and get-off
- Attacks the ball at the catchpoint
- Doesn't bite on double moves, plays with good eye discipline
- Not afraid to get active in the run game
- Was a very bad tackler in 2024, drastically improved in 2025


### Cons
- Limited experience 
- Mirroring skills are eh
- Arm tackler in open space
- Doesn't have a true 'second gear' needed for the fastest receivers
- Grabby when he gets beat (needs to trust his speed more)

## Malik Muhammed
Texas, 6'0 188, 21 Y/O

### Pros
- 3 year contributor 
- Position versatility (can play in the slot and box) 
- Fluid hips 
- Great footwork
- Disciplined eyes
- Physical at the catch point 
- Not scared to get involved in the run game 
- Good understanding of when to pass off routes in zone coverage

### Cons

- Tackling form needs improvement 
- 18.5% missed tackle rate in coverage (2025)

## Jermod McCoy 
Tennessee, 5'11 193, 20 Y/O

### Pros
- Twitched up athlete
- ELITE hip flexibility, can quickly adjust to even the sharpest angles
- Extremely instinctive player
- Great tackler, plays really physical
- Elite deceleration skills are showcased on comebacks/stops
- Great speed to keep up with vertical routes
- Eraser in press man
- Disciplined feet in press
- Good ball skills, very physical at the catch point

### Cons
- Needs to get his head turned around on fades earlier/more often
- Want to see more aggresiveness with hands at the line of scrimmage
- Missed all of 2025 recovering from an ACL tear
- Struggled against Jeremiah Smith
- Height limits his effectiveness against big body X's

## Avieon Terrell
Clemson, (5'11 180 21 Y/O)

### Pros
- Extremely fluid hips
- Great long speed
- Very disciplined eyes in man coverage
- Well developed peanut punch (8 forced fumbles the last 2 years, 5 in 2025)
- Great acceleration after opening his hips up 
- Very good zone coverage in the flat
- Regularly gets his head turned around to make a play on the ball
- Can play inside and outside

### Cons
- Very undersized 
- Struggles against quick releases across his face 
- Hand skills are not good enough to make up for his size
- Below average deceleration 
- Struggles breaking up contested catches against bigger receivers
- Tackling technique needs refinement
