## David Bailey
Texas Tech, 22, 6'3 250 33in arms
Comparisons: Micah Parsons, Chop Robinson
### Pros
- best pure pass rusher in class-not close 
- very explosive first step
- strong hands
- elite bend
- great ghost move
- can drop into zone with great coverage instincts
- good spin move
- full pass rush bag
- extremely athletic
- wins with speed
- great range and pursuit
- can accelerate as he bends 
- good long-arm move
### Cons
- very weak in run defense
- small frame
- struggles setting the edge in run defense
- there are times where he overpursues and leaves cutback lines NFL rb's will expose
- benefits from playing with the best DLine in college

## Rueben Bain
Miami 6'3 270, 21 Y/O, 31in arms 

### Pros
- 21 Y/O
- 6'3 270
- 24% pass rush win rate (PFF)
- Great bend -Rushes with a plan 
- Great spin move 
- Knockout power like Mike Tyson 
- Relentless motor 
- Good instincts -
- Can play in the interior 
- Low center of gravity 
- Great at using his hands 
- Great at anchoring 
- Great at crossing tackles faces and rushing the B gap 
- Great at blowing up blocks and making the tackle, and just tackling the ball-carrier through the blocker

### Cons
- Historically short arms for a top 10 prospect 
- Lower body injury history 
- Not the greatest open field tackler 
- Not a true burner off the edge 
- Gets neutralized when tackles are able to get their hands on him first 
- No NFL EDGE with arms his size have ever had a 10+ sack season or made a pro bowl

## Keldric Faulk
Auburn, 6'6 280, 20 Y/O

- Huge size
- Very versatile player, can play all over the line, even over center
- High level run defender
- Wins with power
- Long arms
- Great setting the edge
- Amazing anchoring technique
- Sheds blockers with ease against the run
- Great at chasing down outside runs from the backside before they are able to develop

- Sack production

- Not very twitched up
- Lacks bend and speed on the edge
- Only has a 9.5%m PRWR against P4 schools in 2025
