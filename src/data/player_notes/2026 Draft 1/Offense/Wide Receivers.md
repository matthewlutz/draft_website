## Carnell Tate
Ohio State, 20, 6'3 195, 4.6
Natural Z receiver, can play X, contested catch with good route running
Comparisons - Tee Higgins, Chris Godwin, Davante Adams, Calvin Ridley
### Pros
 - tall physical receiver 
 - amazing hands with an absurd contested catch rate 
 - 0 drops
 - can win downfield 
 - polished route runner
 - exceptional body control
 - can sell deep and sink hips with purpose on comebacks
 - high iq
 - plucks balls away out of his frame (George Pickens)
 - high level ball tracking
 - although not a big YAC threat he goes North/South with purpose and falls forward consistently
### Cons
- not a big YAC threat
- run blocking technique
- high end breakaway speed is adequate, but not elite
- gets redirected at LOS too much, needs to add some strength

## Jordyn Tyson
Arizona State, 21, 6'2 200, 4.54
Can play slot and boundary, effective at all 3 levels
Comparisons - Jerry Jeudy, Stefon Diggs, Golden Tate, Ceedee Lamb, Cooper Kupp
### Pros
- strong hands, doesn't drop a lot
- almost always makes first tackler miss
- very effective with screens
- very polished as a route runner
- great tracking skills while maintaining speed
- runs angry after the catch
- high end speed
- shows up in the clutch
### Cons
- subpar contested catcher
- runs east/west too much
- injury history
- runs backwards a lot trying to make plays happen
- mistimes jumps sometimes
- needs to use blockers better while running, tries making too much happen on his own

## Chris Bell
Louisville, 23, 6'2 220, 4.44
Very athletic X receiver, still kind of raw
Comparisons - DK Metcalf, AJ Brown, Xavier Legette
### Pros
- great recognition sitting in zones
- need multiple people to tackle him in open field
- back-shoulder beast
- great YAC
- maintains speed throughout the catch
- accelerates quickly
- 50/50 balls are 70/30
### Cons
- route running needs refinement
- want to see more subtleties selling routes
- want to see him use his hands more when catching
- struggles against physical corners

## Zachariah Branch 
Georgia, 21, 5'10 180, 4.35
Extremely fast and shifty slot receiver
Comparisons - Rondale Moore

### Pros
- very elusive
- fastest player on the field
- quick twitch
- can stop on a dime
### Cons
- size
- route running lacks nuances
- poor run blocker, effort is there though

## KC Concepcion
Texas A&M, 5'11 190, 21 Y/O

### Pros
- Very flexible hips allow him to break at sharp angles
- Absolutely ELECTRIC after the catch
- Press man burner
- Very polished route runner
- Catches with hands outside of his frame
- Great football IQ, can find soft spots in zone with ease and punish with YAC
- Very savvy route runner, specifically with head fakes and body leans
- Maintains speed at the top of his route

### Cons
- 10.3% drop rate in 2025
- Average catch radius
- Size/length
- Subpar run blocker
- Although very good after the catch, he does not possess home run speed at the next level
